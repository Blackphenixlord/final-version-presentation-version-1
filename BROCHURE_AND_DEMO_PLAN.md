# DSLM Brochure And Demo Plan

## Brochure URL

https://hunch.nasa.gov/dslm-demo

## Printable Trifold Route

- App route: /trifold
- Purpose: print-ready brochure view for PDF export, review packets, and live presentation handouts.

## What Is In The Project

- Supply-chain workflow starts at vendors and stays visible through receive, tag, pack, stow, crew use, and trash disposal.
- X-400 and X-500 messaging are available in the Ground command-center screen.
- Active RFID tags are tracked live in the Ground command-center screen.
- Crew trash handling includes an auto-scan mode for the trash can inlet.
- The crew header uses a distinct astronaut-facing color treatment.
- The Ground command center includes a demo-process simulation and a lightweight 2-D loading/unloading animation.

## CTB Dimensions To State Clearly

- Width: 48 cm across the hatch-facing side.
- Depth: 40 cm from front handle to rear wall.
- Height: 33 cm from base plate to top lid, excluding soft labels.

## RFID Scanner Placement

- Receive station: handheld RFID scanner at vendor intake bench.
- Tag station: fixed RFID pad beside manifest verification screen.
- Pack station: dual scanner setup for inside and outside CTB pairing.
- Stow station: shelf-side scanner mounted at rack entrance.
- Crew workstation: scanner at Node 2 workstation B for take and return flows.
- Trash can rim: short-range reader at Trash Station A for auto-scan on disposal.

## Other RFID-Capable Devices

- Dedicated USB RFID readers in keyboard-wedge mode.
- Android phones or tablets with NFC for short-range validation.
- Industrial handhelds with integrated RFID sleds.
- BLE-connected mobile readers for walkthrough scans.

## Launch And Cargo Notes

- Expect vibration and transient acceleration during liftoff.
- Immobilize fragile cargo inside the CTB with positive retention and padding.
- Re-verify RFID readability after packout because shielding and orientation can reduce read quality.

## Hayes Review Notes

- Three IMS supply-chain teams can review in Florida or join virtually with the same command-center feed.
- Use the simulated process reel for safety review before live demos.
- Use the in-app 2-D loading/unloading animation as the current stand-in for a fully rendered video.
